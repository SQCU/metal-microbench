# KV retention ⊥ connection lifecycle — root-cause + staged fix (2026-06)

> From the kv-retention-decoupling workflow (9 agents, file:line-grounded, adversarially checked). Bug: <100 streaming requests → ~100x slowdown that never recovers. Operator thesis: a connection is a TRANSPORT fact; a KV page's value is a CONTENT fact; they must not share a retention mechanism.

## Thesis
A connection is a TRANSPORT fact (deliver these bytes to this socket; replay them on reconnect); a KV page's value is a CONTENT fact (will this token prefix be re-prefilled?). These are categorically distinct and must NOT share a retention mechanism. The radix trie (radix_trie.swift) is ALREADY pure content-addressed end to end — adoption (findLongestPrefix 336-461), promotion (insertAnchor/insertPartialAnchor 227-246), partitioning (CvecAnchorTag+imageDigest 24-61), eviction-unlink (invalidateAnchorFor via anchorByPhys 471-496) carry zero connection identity; reuse-value ranking exists and is correct (PageInfo.lastAccessTick page_manager.swift:88-91, bumped on touch 357-361/370-381, plus the LRU-not-MRU forced-eviction scan 326-331). The wrong equation is 'a session references this page' == 'this page is unevictable', in exactly two places: (1) the bridge BRIDGE_CANCEL_ON_DISCONNECT='1' default (server/bridge.py:555) re-couples engine KV teardown to the TCP socket, contradicting the operator directive 3 lines above (506-525); (2) refcount-pinned pages are categorically excluded from the LRU scan, and sessions live in residentSessions indefinitely (lm_engine.swift:695), so any page a resident-but-idle session lists vetoes content reuse-value. HOWEVER, the critic is correct and verified against code: the naive 'demote refcount to a per-CB activeStepPin and evict any activeStepPin==0 page' is UNSOUND because (a) the slide-divergent CoW gate at lm_engine.swift:1270 reads pageRefcount(phys)>1 fed by exactly the adoption increfs at 1219-1220 — removing or repurposing that count silently reintroduces the 16b5416 cross-session slide-K/V clobber AND underflows decref at closeSession (line 2290 decrefs every ownedPages entry); (b) an activeStepPin is scoped to the <=B scheduled slots, so the M-B mid-generation-but-unscheduled sessions would have activeStepPin==0 on all their pages and become eviction-eligible between their own CBs — a corruption class strictly worse than 16b5416; (c) the 're-adopt/re-prefill on eviction' recovery the prior design called 'already built' DOES NOT EXIST (verified: the sole onPageEvicted consumer is the trie unlink at lm_engine.swift:1860; there is no phys->session reverse map and no per-session eviction-dirty mark). THEREFORE the corrected end-state SEPARATES three distinct page-protection facts that the old single refcount conflated: (i) a TRANSIENT active-step pin (the <=B slots writing/reading KV in the command buffer currently dispatched) for in-flight-write safety; (ii) a CONTENT 'listed-by-N-sessions' count, KEPT as a distinct surviving field, used ONLY for CoW soundness (the slide-divergent gate); (iii) a 'wants-slot' liveness bit on resident sessions that protects mid-generation KV from eviction without being a connection signal. Eviction eligibility becomes content-ranked (lastAccessTick) over pages that are neither active-step-pinned NOR listed by any session whose state.wantsSlot — never over a TCP socket. The honest scope: eviction ELIGIBILITY can be made connection-free, but eviction SAFETY (CoW) legitimately retains a session-lifetime listing count; the purity thesis is reachable for the residency veto, not for the CoW gate.

## Page-pin model (the 3-way split that replaces the overloaded refcount)
Three distinct protections replace the one overloaded refcount; do NOT collapse them. (1) TRANSIENT active-step pin (activeStepPin): the count of in-flight forward passes touching a page in the command buffer currently dispatched. Per CB it is the union, over the at-most-B scheduled kernel slots, of the irreducible-transient set — for WRITES, ownedPages[pos/PAGE_FULL] and ownedPages[pos/PAGE_SLIDE] for positions in the tile committed this step; for READS, the covered full prefix ownedPages[0..pos/PAGE_FULL] (full layers) and the slide-window-covered slide pages. CRITICAL GEOMETRY: the pin MUST span BOTH the full-attn ordinal AND the slide ordinal for every tile position (one flat block_table serves PAGE_FULL=8 + PAGE_SLIDE=16; the 16b5416 aliasing seam) or the kernel reads/writes garbage. ORDERING HAZARD (critic-flagged, must fix): all B slots must acquire their pins BEFORE any allocFresh in the same CB-build, or slot j's forced eviction can reclaim a page slot i has pinned-for-read-but-not-yet-read this batch. So pin acquisition is a single atomic pass over all scheduled slots at the slot-assignment site (lm_engine.swift:2421-2429) that precedes every ensurePages/allocFresh (1888-1892) in that CB-build; release the instant gEngineLock releases the CB. activeStepPin governs EVICTION ELIGIBILITY only. (2) LISTING count (kept as a SEPARATE surviving field, NOT merged into activeStepPin): the number of resident sessions that list a page in ownedPages — the OLD refcount semantics, incremented at adoption (1219-1220) and at allocFresh-growth, decremented at closeSession (2290). It is used ONLY for the CoW soundness decision: the slide-divergent gate (lm_engine.swift:1268-1270) and the partial-tail CoW must trigger on 'another session LISTS this page' (listing>1), never on activeStepPin, or two sessions at activeStepPin==0 both skip CoW and clobber the shared slide-divergent half (audit #8). This is acknowledged surviving connection-lifetime state — its purpose is correctness of a divergent WRITE, not retention. (3) WANTS-SLOT residency protection: a resident session whose state.wantsSlot is true is mid-generation; its listed pages are protected from eviction even when activeStepPin==0 this CB. This is what prevents the catastrophic 'evict an unscheduled live session's KV between its CBs' break. It keys on generation-wantedness (a content/compute fact), NOT on any socket. The mid-CoW source+dest pin during copyPhysPageKV (1272/1332-1334) and the fresh-alloc-not-yet-in-ownedPages window (freshHead between its alloc and the freshTail alloc, 1311-1326) both become explicit activeStepPins acquired at alloc time (NOT deferred to slot-assignment, which is too late — critic-flagged gap), released when appended to ownedPages or on bail (the existing decref-on-bail at 1326 is the manual stand-in).

## Eviction policy
Pure reuse-value over prefixes, ranked by lastAccessTick (already correct: field page_manager.swift:88-91, bumped 357-361/370-381, LRU scan 326-331 fixed audit #7), with ZERO connection input. The eviction-eligible set is: {pages with activeStepPin==0 AND not listed by any session whose state.wantsSlot}. This is the CORRECTED policy — NOT the unsound 'any activeStepPin==0 page'. Excluding wants-slot-listed pages is what keeps the M-B mid-generation-but-unscheduled sessions safe; their KV is wanted compute, not cold content. Mechanically this requires the net-new phys->listing-sessions reverse map (does not exist today — verified) so the LRU scan can ask, for a candidate cold page, 'is any live wants-slot session listing you?'. The scan still ranks by lastAccessTick and still fires onPageEvicted (page_manager.swift:341-343) -> RadixTrie.invalidateAnchorFor (radix_trie.swift:471-496) on reclaim — KEEP verbatim. What becomes evictable that is not today: pages listed ONLY by sessions that are NOT wants-slot (i.e. .done, or idle/abandoned generations that have finished) — exactly the 'dead/idle connection pins capacity' pathology, now reclaimable by reuse-value. What stays protected: every page an actively-generating session needs, via wants-slot, plus every page being written/read this CB, via activeStepPin. No wallclock, no per-session quota, no 'session resident' bit, no consumer-liveness ever enters the ranking. The slide-divergent CoW soundness is governed by the separate listing count, not by eviction eligibility. NOTE the residual coupling honestly: wants-slot is a session-lifetime property, so eviction eligibility is 'content-ranked but veto-able by live-generation-wantedness' — which is correct (you must not evict KV a running generation will read), and is categorically different from the old bug (veto-able by an idle/dead socket).

## Request lifecycle
adopt-longest-prefix -> alloc tail -> generate (transient active-step pin) -> promote finished pages -> done (owns nothing). (1) ADOPT-LONGEST-PREFIX: on submit, findLongestPrefix (radix_trie.swift:336-461) returns the deepest content+cvec/image-tag-matching prefix; the session lists those physical pages, increments the LISTING count (kept — feeds CoW soundness), and bumps lastAccessTick (a content touch). It takes NO eviction veto beyond the wants-slot residency protection it already carries as a live generation. (Note: the prior design's 'incref-free read-view adoption' is REJECTED — it underflows the closeSession decref-all at 2290 and disables the CoW gate at 1270; adoption KEEPS its incref, now understood as the listing-count increment, balanced by the closeSession decref.) (2) ALLOC-TAIL: allocFresh only the divergent tail past the adopted prefix, plus the slide-divergent half CoW (1265-1283) and partial-tail CoW (1302-1345), gated on listing>1; these private pages carry an activeStepPin only while being filled. (3) GENERATE: each CB, the scheduler atomically activeStepPins all B scheduled slots' active write/read pages (full+slide ordinals for each tile) BEFORE any allocFresh, runs the forward pass, releases at CB completion. The request's per-active-generation state persists across CBs because state.wantsSlot is true (generation wanted) — not because a socket exists; wants-slot also protects its listed pages from eviction between CBs. (4) PROMOTE FINISHED PAGES: as full-pairs fill, promoteFinishedPages publishes to the trie (lm_engine.swift:2304-2360); the prompt-boundary partial is published via insertPartialAnchor/markPairContentIndexed at the prefill boundary (2247-2285) — warmth is committed to the content cache HERE, not at teardown. (5) DONE-OWNS-NOTHING: on .done/EOS/maxtokens (strictly doneReason set — NEVER 'idle', critic-flagged) or the forward-progress stall-KILL, state.wantsSlot becomes false, the session is unbound from transport and dropped from residency, and closeSession decrefs the listing count so its tail pages fall to listing==0/activeStepPin==0 on freeCached with contentHash intact (page_manager.swift:418-440) — fully adoptable and now genuinely evictable by reuse-value. closeSession (2238-2296) keeps its promote-then-decref-all sequence verbatim; the change is only that pages were never vetoing eviction via a SOCKET, so there is nothing to 'reclaim from a connection.'

## What Session becomes
Session does NOT disappear; it SHRINKS to an ephemeral per-active-generation compute context that owns NO socket-coupled page veto, but DOES retain (a) a listing-count contribution per ownedPages entry (for CoW soundness) and (b) a wants-slot residency protection over those pages while generating. It retains the irreducible per-active-generation state both audits enumerate: ownedPages (now understood as a content-reference list whose pages are protected by wants-slot, not by a connection) + slide/full geometry (position, num_pages_slide/full, k_len_slide/full), nextGeneratedInput tail, inChannelBlock, cot grammar, numGenerated, consumedTokens prefix-hash history, and the cvec/control/detector/trigger steering (activeControls, detectors, triggers, logitBiasDense, minP, stopSequences) — all bound to the in-flight generation, none to a connection. Session.slot (lm_engine.swift:684-697/2421-2429) is ALREADY correctly per-CB transient and stays unchanged — it is the model the design generalizes from (ownership decoupled from slot occupancy, M>>B). What LEAVES the Session: its role as a connection-lifetime retention anchor. residentSessions stops meaning 'resident indefinitely for warmth' (delete the rationale at 695); membership now means 'a generation currently wanted / schedulable' (state.wantsSlot), which confers scheduling candidacy AND mid-generation page protection, but never socket-coupled residency. Once .done, wants-slot is false, the Session owns nothing schedulable and is dropped (2294). lm_session.swift (the v1 single-tenant LmSession.generate benchmarking path) is UNAFFECTED — no refcount, no residency, no reapers, fixed per-slot block_table for one generate() call.

## Warmth & reconnect (both need zero page pinning)
Both are satisfied with ZERO page pinning because they live in two already-separable systems, neither of which is the engine Session. WARMTH = the content cache. closeSession PUBLISHES the prefix to the radix trie (insertPartialAnchor/markPairContentIndexed at the prompt boundary lm_engine.swift:2247-2285; promoteFinishedPages full-pairs 2304-2360) BEFORE releasing pages, and decref PRESERVES contentHash (page_manager.swift:418-440), so a finished session's pages sit at listing==0/activeStepPin==0 on freeCached with hash intact, ranked by lastAccessTick, adopted read-only by the next identical request via findLongestPrefix + incref-resurrection (radix_trie.swift:336-461, page_manager.swift:370-381). Warmth survives session death and needs zero resident session and zero pin; a pinned-for-warmth session pins real slot+page capacity for ZERO warmth benefit. RECONNECT = transport-log replay. GET /v1/streams/{id}/sse?since=N -> stream_reconnect_sse reads ONLY _stream_logs[stream_id]/_response_qs (bridge.py:2427-2487/402-501); it NEVER re-submits to or re-attaches a live engine session, and the 404 gate tests LOG existence not session existence (2441-2444) — proof the two are separable, merely keyed by one stream_id. So reconnect needs the retained append-log (a transport buffer), NOT a pinned engine session, which may be long .done. CONFIRMED: neither warmth nor reconnect requires page pinning, and neither takes any connection signal into the KV-residency decision. THEREFORE on disconnect: always leave the append-log accruing for reconnect (transport); let the engine continue or finish on its OWN policy (forward-progress deadline / explicit /cancel / admission-pressure — never is_disconnected); treat warmth as already committed to the trie. One caveat the critic surfaces and this design accepts: a disconnected-but-continuing generation is a wants-slot session, so its KV IS protected from eviction by the wants-slot rule — exactly as a connected generation would be — without any socket signal; if it runs away with no consumer, the admission-pressure/cancel backstop (not is_disconnected) sheds it.

## Minimal vs full (staged)
SMALLEST change that DISSOLVES the bug class (the <100-req / ~100x / never-recovers slowdown) WITHOUT introducing a new corruption class — Stage 0, two no-kernel steps the critic blesses for immediate rebuild: (A) server/bridge.py:555 — default BRIDGE_CANCEL_ON_DISCONNECT to '0', so a TCP disconnect stops tearing down the engine session (decouples connection lifetime from engine KV teardown; bit-identical outputs, only retention timing). (B, .done-ONLY variant) lm_engine.swift — on a session reaching strictly .done (doneReason set; NEVER 'idle'/'unscheduled'), run the existing promotion (2243-2285) then decref ownedPages promptly so pages fall to trie-backed freeCached at generation-done rather than waiting on connection end (it already does this at closeSession via 2290 + page_manager.swift:418-440; the residual work is firing closeSession at generation-done, already detected by the pump u.state==2), AND delete the 'resident indefinitely for warmth' rationale (695). Why this dissolves the class: the pathology is 'a dead/idle connection's pages are an absolute eviction veto' (page_manager.swift:326-331/437-439 + sessions resident indefinitely, 695). Once dead/idle sessions stop pinning, allocFresh no longer hits outOfPages or force-evicts hotter shared pages with no reaper to reclaim a live-but-idle resident; warm prefixes survive in the trie; the lastAccessTick LRU is no longer short-circuited for finished work. (A)+(B) mean dead/idle connections pin zero pool capacity and the pool can always reclaim the coldest FINISHED content. The header even admits the gap (page_manager.swift:30-35). WHAT IS EXPLICITLY DEFERRED (critic verdict: NOT safe to rebuild as the prior design wrote it): incref-free adoption (REJECTED outright — underflows decref-all at 2290 and disables the CoW gate at 1270); 'evict any activeStepPin==0 page' (REJECTED — makes the M-B unscheduled live sessions eviction-eligible mid-generation, corruption strictly worse than 16b5416); the 're-prefill on eviction' recovery (does NOT exist — net-new phys->session reverse map + eviction-dirty mark + re-prefill path, the single largest unstated build cost). FULL ideal end-state, only after that net-new machinery exists: SPLIT refcount into activeStepPin + listingCount; eviction-eligible = activeStepPin==0 AND not listed by any wants-slot session (NOT 'any activeStepPin==0'); atomic per-CB pin across all B slots before any allocFresh; CoW gate reads listingCount; Session shrunk to per-active-generation context; retention reapers + residency-for-warmth deleted; bridge LRU transport-only; admission-pressure backstop added. RECOMMENDED STAGED PATH (each independently shippable + reversible): STAGE 0 = ship (A) + .done-only (B) under flags, validate with LM_KV_OVERWRITE_CHECK=1, the conc8 deterministic repro (17.7%->1% method), a <100-req soak that must no longer hit outOfPages and must recover, PLUS a disconnect-storm smoke of the revived =0-branch machinery and a fast-runaway-no-consumer test; STAGE 1 = build the phys->listing-sessions reverse map + per-session eviction-dirty mark + re-prefill-before-read path (no eviction-scope change yet — pure machinery, validated in isolation); STAGE 2 = split refcount into activeStepPin+listingCount and widen the eviction scan to {activeStepPin==0 AND not wants-slot-listed}, with atomic per-CB pin ordering (touches the 16b5416 slide-geometry seam — gate behind CoW-soundness + absorbing-state-collapse repro); STAGE 3 = delete the now-dead retention reapers, reframe the bridge LRU transport-only, revisit MAX_RESIDENT_SESSIONS, add admission-pressure cancel. Stage 0 alone removes the pathology and needs no kernel changes; everything past it requires the reverse-map machinery to exist FIRST.

## Risks
- PRIMARY (Stage 2): eviction of an actively-generating page. The naive 'evict any activeStepPin==0 page' makes every mid-generation-but-unscheduled session (M-B of them) eviction-eligible, since activeStepPin is scoped to the <=B scheduled slots. Force-eviction reallocates such a page, overwrites contentHash, fires onPageEvicted to unlink the TRIE anchor — but the OWNING session's ownedPages[k] still indexes that phys, now backing another session's KV: cross-session corruption strictly worse than 16b5416 (which was bounded to the slide-divergent half of ADOPTED pages). MITIGATION IN THIS DESIGN: eviction-eligible excludes pages listed by any wants-slot session, not merely activeStepPin==0. MUST validate with LM_KV_OVERWRITE_CHECK=1 AND a conc>B soak, not just conc8.
- Session-side dangling ownedPages has NO invalidation today (asymmetry bug): the trie gets onPageEvicted->invalidateAnchorFor (radix_trie.swift:471-496) but the session does not. There is NO phys->listing-sessions reverse map and NO per-session eviction-dirty mark (verified: sole onPageEvicted consumer is the trie unlink at lm_engine.swift:1860). Stage 2 is blocked on building (a) the reverse map, (b) on-eviction mark of each listing session's affected position range, (c) re-allocFresh + re-prefill of exactly those positions before any read. This is net-new code, the largest unstated build cost; do NOT widen the eviction scan before it exists.
- Slide-divergent CoW gate weakening: the gate at lm_engine.swift:1270 reads pageRefcount(phys)>1, fed by the adoption increfs at 1219-1220. If refcount is repurposed to activeStepPin, two sessions listing the same slide-divergent page both see <=1 and BOTH skip CoW -> cross-session slide-K/V clobber -> multilingual-token-soup absorbing-state collapse (audit #8). MUST keep listingCount as a DISTINCT surviving field for the CoW decision; do NOT merge it into activeStepPin. Deterministic only under concurrency — a single clean run is untrustworthy (kv_slide_full_geometry memory).
- Stage 1 incref-free adoption is REJECTED, not deferred: removing the increfs at 1219-1220 underflows the closeSession decref-all at lm_engine.swift:2290 (it decrefs every ownedPages entry) on EVERY adopting session's teardown, AND the slide-divergent CoW decref at 1273 decrefs a never-increfed page -> decref-of-free-page balance bug (page_manager.swift:444-449). Adoption KEEPS its incref (the listing count).

## Critic verdict
SHIP STAGE 0 (A) ONLY as the immediate rebuild-and-deploy. DO NOT ship Stage 1, Stage 2, or Stage 0 (B) as specified — they are not safe to rebuild+ship and need more design work. Detailed: Stage 0 (A) — flipping BRIDGE_CANCEL_ON_DISCONNECT default to '0' — is a pure-Python, no-kernel, bit-identical-output change that removes the dominant re-coupling seam (verified: bridge.py:555 fires action=2->closeSession on every TCP drop, directly contradicting the operator directive at 506-525). It dissolves the documented <100-req/never-recovers pathology because the pathology is 'dead/idle connections' pages are an absolute eviction veto' and (A) stops connections from tearing down OR pinning beyond their natural generation. Gate (A) behind: LM_KV_OVERWRITE_CHECK=1, a conc8 deterministic repro (the 17.7->1% method), AND a >100-req soak that must not hit outOfPages and must recover; ALSO smoke the now-revived =0-branch machinery (background drain, _active_consumer_token one-reader gate, bounded log LRU) under a disconnect storm, plus add/verify an admission-pressure or explicit-cancel backstop for fast-runaway no-consumer sessions that expireStalledSessions cannot catch (it only reaps non-advancing sessions). Stage 0 (B) is NOT safe as written: it must be restricted to strictly .done (never 'idle'), and even then it only relocates the existing closeSession decref earlier — acceptable, but only the .done variant. Stage 1 is UNSAFE: incref-free adoption is internally inconsistent with the unchanged decref-all-ownedPages teardown (refcount underflow on every adopter close) AND silently disables the 16b5416 slide-divergent CoW (the >1 gate is fed by exactly the increfs Stage 1 removes). Stage 2 is the dangerous one and is NOT safe to rebuild on: (i) 'evict any activeStepPin==0 page' makes every unscheduled live session (M-B of them) eviction-eligible mid-generation, a corruption class strictly worse than 16b5416; (ii) the recovery mechanism the design calls 'already-built' (re-adopt/re-prefill mark) DOES NOT EXIST — there is no phys->listing-sessions reverse map and no per-session eviction-dirty flag; only the trie gets an eviction callback, the session does not; this is substantial net-new code, not a re-wiring; (iii) the CoW soundness gate forces retention of the OLD listing-count semantics as a separate surviving counter, so 'demote refcount to activeStepPin everywhere' is false and the design's purity thesis is only partially reachable. RECOMMENDATION: rebuild on Stage 0 (A) + the .done-only variant of (B). Treat Stages 1-3 as requiring a redesign that (1) keeps a distinct listing-count field for CoW soundness, (2) builds the phys->session reverse map + eviction-dirty mark + re-prefill path BEFORE widening the eviction scan, (3) makes pin acquisition atomic across all B slots before any allocFresh within a CB-build, and (4) constrains the eviction-eligible set to exclude pages listed by any session in residentSessions whose state.wantsSlot, not merely activeStepPin==0. The minimal staged fix's Stage 0(A) is safe and high-leverage; the 'full' design as written would corrupt KV across live sessions and relies on recovery machinery that is not present.

### Critic — breaks
- STAGE 2 BREAKS THE M>>B DESIGN, NOT JUST EDGE CASES: The eviction policy 'eviction-eligible = any page with activeStepPin==0, ranked by lastAccessTick' is catastrophic precisely because activeStepPin is, by the design's own definition, scoped to 'the <=B kernel slots in the command buffer currently dispatched.' With M resident generating sessions and only B<=16 scheduled per CB, the M-B sessions that are mid-generation but not scheduled THIS CB have activeStepPin==0 on EVERY page they own. They become eviction-eligible. Their KV (already-written prefix + generation tail) can be reclaimed and handed to a fresh allocFresh between two of their own CBs. lastAccessTick only ranks them as 'cold' relative to the hotter scheduled few; under pool pressure the policy will evict actively-generating-but-waiting sessions' KV. The current code's refcount-veto is the ONLY thing preventing this, and the design proposes to delete exactly that veto. This is not the 'idle dead connection' case the design targets; it is a live, wanted, mid-generation session whose compute context is destroyed.
- STAGE 2 BREAKS slide/full geometry coverage as specified: the design says the per-CB activeStepPin must cover 'WRITES: ownedPages[pos/PAGE_FULL] and ownedPages[pos/PAGE_SLIDE] for the tile; READS: full prefix ownedPages[0..pos/PAGE_FULL] and slide-window-covered pages.' But this pin set is ONLY the CURRENTLY-SCHEDULED slot's working set. A slot scheduled this CB for a DEEP context (position 16N) reads ownedPages[0..2N-1] for full layers — that is 2N pages all needing the pin THIS CB. The design's pin set is correct for the scheduled slot, but the eviction set is 'all activeStepPin==0' GLOBALLY, so a DIFFERENT scheduled slot's allocFresh in the same CB-build can evict a page the first slot will read later in the same dispatched batch if pin acquisition is not atomic across all B slot-assignments BEFORE any allocFresh fires. The design pins 'at the slot-assignment site lm_engine.swift:2421-2429' but ensurePages/allocFresh (1888-1892) for slot j can run interleaved with slot i's pin acquisition. Ordering hazard: all B slots must pin BEFORE any allocFresh in the CB-build, or one slot evicts another's just-pinned-but-not-yet read page within one CB.
- STAGE 2 WEAKENS the CoW soundness gate (16b5416) unless the design's own caveat is implemented as a genuinely separate counter — and the design admits this is a NEW counter, not a reuse. The CoW gate at lm_engine.swift:1270 is `pageRefcount(phys) > 1`. Today refcount == listed-by-N-sessions, so the gate correctly means 'another session lists this page, so my divergent slide write would clobber it.' Redefining refcount to activeStepPin makes pageRefcount(phys) return the per-CB pin count (0 or 1 for an idle-listed page), so two sessions listing the same slide-divergent page both see refcount<=1 and BOTH skip CoW -> cross-session slide-K/V clobber = the exact absorbing-state collapse of 16b5416 / audit #8. The design says to keep a separate 'listed-by-N-sessions' query for the CoW decision, but that means the OLD refcount semantics must be PRESERVED IN FULL as a second field — the design does not get to delete the connection-lifetime listing counter; it must keep it for CoW soundness. So 'demote refcount to activeStepPin everywhere' is false: the listing counter must survive, and it is still incremented at adoption and decremented at closeSession, i.e. still session/connection-lifetime-scoped.
- STAGE 1 BREAKS the slide-divergent CoW even before Stage 2: Stage 1 ('convert adoptSharedPrefixPages to an incref-free read-view that only bumps lastAccessTick') removes the incref at lines 1219-1220. But the slide-divergent CoW gate at line 1270 reads pageRefcount(phys) > 1, and that >1 is PRODUCED by exactly those increfs (producer's 1 + each adopter's incref). If adoption stops increfing, an adopted slide-divergent page sits at refcount==1 (producer only, or 0 if producer released), the CoW gate at 1270 never fires, and the adopter writes its divergent slide K/V straight into the producer's shared page. Stage 1 is described as shippable independently of Stage 2 but it silently disables the 16b5416 CoW. This is a correctness regression introduced by the supposedly-safe intermediate stage.

### Critic — correctness hazards
- Eviction of an actively-generating page (PRIMARY HAZARD): Stage 2 makes any page with activeStepPin==0 evictable, which includes every page of every mid-generation session not scheduled in the current CB (M-B of them). Force-eviction will reallocate such a page (allocFresh forced path, page_manager.swift:319-356), overwrite its contentHash, fire onPageEvicted to unlink the trie anchor — but the OWNING session's ownedPages[k] still indexes that phys, now backing a different session's KV. This is cross-session KV corruption strictly worse than 16b5416 (which was bounded to the slide-divergent half of ADOPTED pages; this hits ALL pages of ALL unscheduled live sessions). - Trie anchor pointing at an evicted page is HANDLED for the trie (invalidateAnchorFor unlinks via anchorByPhys, verified radix_trie.swift:471-496, including pairMate at page_manager.swift:342-350) — this part is sound and KEEP-verbatim is correct. The hazard is NOT the trie's dangling anchor; it is the SESSION's dangling ownedPages entry, which has no equivalent invalidation. The asymmetry is the bug: content (trie) gets notified on eviction; the live consumer (session) does not.
- Double-free / refcount underflow via Stage-1+Stage-2 interaction: if Stage 1 makes adoption incref-free (read-view) but the slide-divergent CoW at 1273 still does engine.pageManager.decref(physPage: phys) on the adopted page (it decrefs the page it is replacing), then a never-incref'd page gets decref'd — refcount underflow / decref-of-free-page (page_manager.swift:444-449 logs it as a balance bug). Stage 1 as described ('incref-free read-view') is internally inconsistent with the unchanged decref-all-ownedPages teardown and the CoW decref.
- CoW gate false-negative under activeStepPin (audit #8 regression): covered in breaks, but as a pure correctness hazard: pageRefcount used at 1270 returning the per-CB pin (not the listing count) yields refcount<=1 for genuinely-shared slide-divergent pages -> CoW skipped -> cross-session slide K/V clobber -> multilingual-token-soup absorbing-state collapse. Deterministic only under concurrency; a single clean run is not trustworthy (per kv_slide_full_geometry memory).
- Pool-sizing masquerade: aggressive Stage-2 eviction + (nonexistent) re-prefill, if re-prefill thrash sets in (two competing prefixes evict-then-refill each other every step under pressure), manifests as a kernel-time regression that is actually page-fault/recompute churn — must check vm_stat and cache_hits/cache_misses before attributing to kernels (kv_pool_vs_model_size memory).

_CONFIRMED CONFLATION. Two independent retention systems exist and are correctly separable: (1) TRANSPORT — the bridge append-log + completed-stream LRU (_stream_logs / _completed_stream_lru / since=N replay), which is what reconnect actually consumes; (2) ENGINE — the content-addressed radix-trie prefix cache + refcounted pages (page_manager.swift / radix_trie.swift), which is what 'warmth' actually is and which survives closeSession by design. The directive's framing ('client disconnects no longer cancel the engine session / keep alive for warmth') ties an ENGINE-KV decision (retain the session) to a TRANSPORT event (the TCP connection going away), when reconnect needs only the LOG and warmth needs only the content cache.

Decisive code facts:
- Reconnect path (bridge.py:2441-2487) reads ONLY _stream_logs/_response_qs; never re-submits to engine. 404 = 'log not in dict', never 'session dead'. So reconnect provably needs the LOG, not the live SESSION.
- closeSession (lm_engine.swift:2238-2296) PUBLISHES prefix pages to the trie (insertPartialAnchor/markPairContentIndexed) BEFORE decref. Pages at refcount==0 keep contentHash and stay adoptable (page_manager.swift:87-88, 288-360). So warmth is committed AT close and does NOT need a pinned session.
- The default disconnect policy (bridge.py:555-565, BRIDGE_CANCEL_ON_DISCONNECT defaults to 1) actively CANCELS the engine session and releases the log on disconnect — directly contradicting the 'keep the session alive for warmth' directive and making the whole LRU/reconnect/background-drain machinery inert unless an operator opts in. This is the strongest tell that connection lifecycle is (incoherently) driving engine retention.

What GENUINELY needs engine state to survive a disconnect: essentially nothing once state==2 (warmth is already in the content cache). For a mid-generation disconnect, the only thing worth keeping is the in-flight DECODE itself, and even that is a cache-population convenience (the next request re-prefills against the cached partial prefix), not a correctness requirement — and it should be governed by the engine's own forward-progress deadline (P3, lm_engine.swift:1720) or an explicit /cancel, NOT by the transport connection's liveness.

Recommendation implied (not requested as a change): decouple by (a) letting disconnect always leave the LOG for reconnect [transport concern, the append-log already does this], (b) deciding engine-session continuation on an engine-owned policy (progress deadline / explicit cancel / admission pressure), never on is_disconnected(), and (c) treating 'warmth' as satisfied by the content cache so no session need be pinned past state==2.

Minor: the task prompt's `expireAbandonedSessions` was renamed to `expireStalledSessions` (lm_engine.swift:1711) and is engine-internal stall detection, not the consumer-coupling it might appear to be._
